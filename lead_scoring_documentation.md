# Ministry SaaS Lead Scoring Framework

## Executive Summary

This document outlines the comprehensive lead scoring system for Ministry SaaS Net New Accounts. The scoring model uses a combined fit and engagement score ranging from -2,000 to 2,000 points to prioritize prospects based on their organizational characteristics (fit) and their level of interest and interaction (engagement).

**Score Range**: -2,000 to 2,000 points  
**Purpose**: Identify and prioritize high-value prospects for Account Executive follow-up

---

## Score Interpretation & Actions

### Hot Leads (1,000 - 2,000 points)
- **Action**: Immediate AE assignment
- **Priority**: Highest
- **Characteristics**: Multiple strong fit signals + high engagement + active buying intent

### Warm Leads (500 - 999 points)
- **Action**: Qualified for outreach within 24-48 hours
- **Priority**: High
- **Characteristics**: Good fit signals + moderate engagement or single strong buying signal

### Developing Leads (100 - 499 points)
- **Action**: Nurture campaign enrollment
- **Priority**: Medium
- **Characteristics**: Some fit signals + early-stage engagement

### Cold Leads (0 - 99 points)
- **Action**: Long-term nurture
- **Priority**: Low
- **Characteristics**: Minimal signals or early awareness stage

### Disqualified Leads (-2,000 - -1 points)
- **Action**: Remove from active campaigns
- **Priority**: None
- **Characteristics**: Multiple disqualifying factors or explicit opt-out

## Scoring Logic Summary

The lead scoring model combines two dimensions:

1. **Fit Score**: Based on firmographics, company characteristics, and ideal customer profile alignment
2. **Engagement Score**: Based on behavioral signals, buying intent indicators, and sales qualification factors

**Score Calculation**:
- Each criterion adds or subtracts points based on significance
- Scores are cumulative across all categories
- Higher positive scores indicate better alignment and higher priority
- Negative scores identify disqualifying characteristics or lack of fit
